Looking at different museums and engagement of children. With reasons for family days out, education and a boost to the economy (?), more museums should contain interactive elements. Learning more by interacting instead of just reading a sign or looking at a painting. Beneficial to not just kids, they are optional so do not have to accessed and used to be understood. Gives museums kid friendly places, making them more accessible to more people, some exhibits not having any make more quiet calm spaces for those who desire.


To be looking further into the adults and parents of children who play video games and try to market interactivity to them. Parents and adults who don't count themselves as 'gamers' but would still indulge in playing games on their phones such as candy crush and wordscapes. By finding a level of interactivity that suits them and what they want to achieve in terms of learning.
Making video games in a learning context more accessible for adults, not have them be too difficult or condescending.
